Seoul 8D7N — April 2026

Views, K-culture, food & efficiency. Clustered by neighborhood.

Trip Overview

What to Know

  • Base: Stay in Myeongdong or Jongno for central subway access
  • Transport: Buy T-money card at airport for all subway/bus rides
  • Best time: Early April for cherry blossoms along Han River & Yeouido
  • Pre-book: Changdeokgung Secret Garden, DMZ tour, Seoul Sky tickets

Day 1 — Old Seoul & Night Skyline

Areas: Jongno, Bukchon, Insadong, Namsan

Morning / Arrival

Arrive at Incheon (ICN)

Take AREX Express Train (43 min to Seoul Station) or Airport Limousine Bus to Myeongdong/Jongno hotel. Check in, drop bags, rest.

Buy T-money card at airport convenience store (CU/GS25) for all transport.
12:30 PM

Lunch: Tosokchon Samgyetang

Famous ginseng chicken soup near Gyeongbokgung Palace. Iconic Seoul comfort food — whole chicken stuffed with rice, ginseng, jujube.

Arrive before 1 PM to avoid long lines. Cash preferred.
2:00 PM

Gyeongbokgung Palace

Korea's largest royal palace (1395). Main gate, throne hall, and photogenic pavilions over lotus pond. Allow 1.5 hours.

Changing of the Guard ceremony at 2 PM (10 min). Free entry in hanbok rental.
3:45 PM

Bukchon Hanok Village

Traditional Korean hanok alleyways between palaces. Quiet residential streets with classic architecture and rooftop views.

Walk quietly — locals live here. Best photo spots: Bukchon 8-View viewpoints.
5:00 PM

Insadong Street

Traditional crafts, tea houses, antique shops. Ssamziegil shopping mall has unique Korean souvenirs and rooftop views.

5:45 PM

Jogyesa Temple

Central Seoul's main Buddhist temple. Thousands of colorful lanterns hang year-round. Peaceful 15-minute stop.

7:00 PM

Dinner: Free and easy

Refined hanjeongsik (Korean royal court cuisine) — multi-course meal with small banchan side dishes in traditional setting.

8:30 PM

N Seoul Tower (Namsan)

Taxi to cable car base (10 min) → ride up to tower. 360° night skyline of Seoul from observation deck. Love Lock Terrace.

Buy tower tickets online to skip lines. Last entry 11 PM.
10:30 PM

Myeongdong Night Walk

Quick stroll through famous shopping street. Street food vendors (tteokbokki, hotteok). Return to hotel.

Day 2 — Royal Gardens & Lantern Stream

Areas: Jongno, Ikseondong, Cheonggyecheon

9:30 AM

Changdeokgung Palace + Secret Garden

UNESCO World Heritage palace with stunning Secret Garden (Huwon). Guided tour only — pristine nature, pavilions, lotus ponds. 2 hours.

Pre-book timed tickets online — limited daily slots. English tours at 10:30 AM, 2:30 PM.
12:00 PM

Lunch: Free and easy

1:30 PM

Ikseondong Hanok Street

Hidden gem — maze-like hanok alleys converted to boutique cafes, bakeries, and shops. More intimate than Bukchon. Great for photos.

Stop at Ikseondong Bokdeokbang for artisan Korean tea in renovated hanok.
3:30 PM

Return to Hotel / Rest (Optional)

Short subway ride back to hotel. Rest, recharge for evening activities.

6:00 PM

Cheonggyecheon Stream

8km urban stream through downtown Seoul. Walk from City Hall to Dongdaemun (3 km, ~40 min). Illuminated bridges and art installations at night.

Start at Cheonggye Plaza entrance near City Hall Station (Line 1/2). Walk east toward Dongdaemun.
7:30 PM

Dinner: Free and easy

Day 3 — Modern Seoul & Skyline Luxury

Areas: COEX, Bongeunsa, Seokchon Lake, Lotte World Tower

10:00 AM

COEX Mall & Starfield Library

Asia's largest underground mall. Starfield Library — stunning 13-meter-high open library with 50,000 books. Free entry. Major Instagram spot.

Library is in center of COEX Mall. Station: Samseong (Line 2). Open 10:30 AM–10 PM.
11:15 AM

Bongeunsa Temple

1,200-year-old Buddhist temple in the heart of Gangnam. Stunning contrast — ancient temple with modern skyscrapers behind. Giant Maitreya Buddha statue.

Walk 5 min from COEX across the street. Free entry. Temple stay programs available.
12:30 PM

Lunch: Maple Tree House (Gangnam BBQ)

High-quality Korean BBQ near COEX. Premium cuts of beef and pork grilled at your table with fresh banchan. Modern, clean interior.

2:30 PM

Seokchon Lake Park

Circular walking path around peaceful lake. Cherry blossom hotspot in early April. Lotte World Tower views. Paddleboat rentals available.

Take Line 2 two stops from Samseong to Jamsil Station. 5-min walk to lake.
4:30 PM

Lotte World Tower — Seoul Sky Observatory

5th tallest building in the world. Seoul Sky observatory on floors 117–123 (500+ meters high). 360° sunset/night views. Sky bridge with glass floor.

Pre-book tickets online for specific time slot. Arrive 30 min before sunset (~6:30 PM in April).
7:30 PM

Dinner: Tower or Nearby

Option 1: Dine inside Lotte Tower (multiple restaurants floors 1–80). Option 2: Return to Jamsil area for seafood or Korean BBQ.

Day 4 — K-Pop & Celebrity Seoul + Gangnam Luxury

Areas: Gangdong → Apgujeong → Cheongdam → Sinsa

9:00 AM

JYP Entertainment Building (Gangdong-gu)

Quick 10-minute photo stop outside JYP HQ (home of TWICE, Stray Kids, ITZY). Can't enter building, but great for fans to see the iconic entrance.

Subway: Gangdong Station (Line 5) Exit 4, 5-min walk. Or take taxi directly from hotel.
9:30 AM

Olympic Park (World Peace Gate & Lake)

Relaxing morning stroll through Seoul's beautiful Olympic Park. Walk around the lake, see World Peace Gate sculpture, and enjoy cherry blossoms in early April. Peaceful green space with art installations.

Subway: Olympic Park Station (Line 5) Exit 3. Optional coffee at Coffee Bean with lake view. Allow 1.5 hours for leisurely walk.
11:30 AM

K-Star Road (Apgujeong Rodeo)

Take photos with Gangnam-dol idol statues (BTS, EXO, BLACKPINK, BIGBANG). 1.5km pedestrian-friendly street with K-pop themed sculptures and celebrity agency sightings.

Travel from Olympic Park: Subway or taxi (~25 min). Start at Apgujeong Rodeo Station (Line 7) Exit 2.
12:15 PM

SM TOWN @ COEX Artium or SM Culture Square

Small exhibits, artist merchandise, and photo zones for SM artists (NCT, aespa, Red Velvet, EXO). Located in Starfield COEX Mall. Even if museum is under renovation, area around SM HQ has fun K-pop displays.

Free to browse. Merch shop opens 11 AM. Walking distance from K-Star Road (~10 min) or short subway ride.
1:00 PM

Lunch: Yukjeon Hoekwan (Gangnam BBQ)

Old-school Korean BBQ popular with locals since 1962. Famous for galbi (short ribs) and beef jeon (savory pancakes). Traditional interior with quality meats.

Near Gangnam Station. Moderate prices. Can accommodate larger groups. Cash and card accepted.
2:00 PM

Garosugil Street (Sinsa-dong)

Tree-lined avenue perfect for shopping — mix of streetwear, indie Korean fashion, and beauty stores. More upscale and less crowded than Myeongdong. Great for wife's shopping!

Subway: Sinsa Station (Line 3) Exit 8. Walk along main Garosugil street. Allow 2-3 hours for shopping and exploring.
5:30 PM

Cheongdam Luxury Boutiques (Optional)

If energy permits, walk 15 minutes north to Cheongdam-dong for high-end flagship stores (Dior, Chanel, Hermès). Window shopping and people-watching in Seoul's most upscale neighborhood.

6:00 PM

Dinner: Free and easy

8:30 PM (Optional)

Seoul Wave Art Center or Garosugil Night Stroll

Seoul Wave Art Center — beautiful illuminations on Han River (short ride from Gangnam).
Or simply stroll Garosugil after dinner — the street comes alive at night with cafes, bars, and beautiful lighting.

Day 5 — Hongdae Street Life & Indie Culture

Areas: Hapjeong, Hongdae, Yeonnam-dong

10:30 AM

YG Entertainment HQ (Hapjeong)

Photo stop outside YG building (BLACKPINK, BIGBANG, TREASURE). Can't enter, but popular fan pilgrimage spot. 5 min from Hapjeong Station Exit 8.

11:00 AM

Hongdae Area — Street Art & Markets

Youth culture epicenter — indie music, street art, busking, underground shopping. Weekend Free Market (Sat 1–6 PM) for handmade crafts.

Main pedestrian zone: Hongik University Gate → AK&Hongdae Mall. Vintage shops in alleys.
1:00 PM

Lunch: Free and easy

3:00 PM

Yeonnam-dong Gyeongui Line Forest Park

Converted railway line into linear park. Trendy cafes, brunch spots, boutiques along tree-lined paths. Quieter, more local than Hongdae main drag.

Walk 10 min west from Hongdae Station or take Line 2 one stop to Hongdae/Yeonnam. Park runs 6km north-south.
7:00 PM

Dinner: Free and easy

9:00 PM

Hongdae Night Scene

Stay for live street performances (dance crews, bands). Explore bars, clubs, karaoke (noraebang). Vibrant nightlife until 3 AM+.

Day 6 — DMZ, Seafood Market & River Cruise

Areas: Paju DMZ → Noryangjin → Yeouido → Han River

7:00 AM – 2:00 PM

DMZ Half-Day Tour

Morning pickup from hotel or City Hall area. Visit Imjingak Park → 3rd Infiltration Tunnel → Dora Observatory → Dorasan Station. Returns ~2 PM.

Bring passport (required for security checks). Dress code: no ripped jeans, sleeveless tops, or flip-flops. Book tour 1–2 weeks ahead.
2:30 PM

Late Lunch: Noryangjin Fish Market

Korea's largest seafood auction market. Choose live crab, fish, or shellfish from vendors. Take upstairs to restaurants who cook it for you (small fee). Authentic local experience.

Take Line 1 to Noryangjin Station, 5 min walk. Bargain respectfully. Bring cash. Try raw fish (hoe) or spicy stew (maeuntang).
5:00 PM

Yeouido Han River Park

Seoul's riverside park with cherry blossom paths (peak early April). Rent bikes, picnic areas, convenience stores. Relax before cruise.

Subway: Yeouido Station (Line 5) Exit 3. Walk 10 min to river. Cherry blossom peak: April 1–10.
7:00 PM

E-Land Han River Cruise

Sunset or night cruise departing from Yeouido Pier. 1-hour scenic ride past Banpo Bridge Rainbow Fountain, 63 Building, Lotte Tower. Live music onboard.

Book tickets online or at pier booth. Sunset departure ~6:30 PM (April). Bring light jacket — breezy on deck.
8:30 PM

Dinner: 63 Building Restaurants (optional)

Buffet or fine dining on upper floors of 63 Building (gold glass skyscraper). Floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking Han River and city lights.

Day 7 — Hidden Views & Final Shopping

Areas: Eungbongsan → Seoul Forest → Myeongdong

9:00 AM

Eungbongsan Mountain Sunrise Viewpoint

Easy 10-minute hike up small mountain for 360° Seoul skyline view over Han River. Less touristy than Namsan. Local favorite.

Subway: Eungbong Station (Line 2) Exit 4. Follow trail signs uphill. Octagonal pavilion at peak. Bring coffee from nearby cafe.
10:30 AM

Seoul Forest

Large urban park with deer enclosure, butterfly conservatory, picnic areas, and Han River views. Modern, well-maintained. Popular with families.

Subway: Seoul Forest Station (Line 2 or Bundang Line). 2 km from Eungbongsan — 20 min walk or quick subway. Park open 24/7, free.
1:00 PM

Lunch: free and easy

Can see what's around.

3:00 PM

Final Shopping: Myeongdong

Seoul's shopping mecca. K-beauty flagships (Innisfree, Etude House, The Face Shop, Olive Young mega-store). Cosmetics, fashion, souvenirs.

Tax refund available — shop at stores with "Tax Free" signs. Bring passport. Subway: Myeongdong Station (Line 4) Exit 6.
5:00 PM

Lotte Department Store (Main Branch)

12-floor luxury department store in Myeongdong. Designer brands, food hall basement (gourmet Korean snacks), rooftop garden. One-stop gift shopping.

7:30 PM

Dinner: Free and easy

Jihwaja: Upscale Buddhist temple cuisine in traditional hanok. Vegetarian, refined. Baekjeong: Premium Korean BBQ chain with quality meats.

9:00 PM

Final Cheonggyecheon Night Walk

One last stroll along the illuminated stream. Reflect on trip. Return to hotel and pack for departure.

Day 8 — Departure

Airport transfer & departure

Morning

Hotel Breakfast & Check-out

Final Korean breakfast near hotel. Check out by 11 AM. Store luggage at hotel if flight is later.

3 hours before flight

Airport Transfer

Take AREX Express (43 min) or Airport Bus from Myeongdong/Jongno to Incheon Airport. Allow 3 hours door-to-gate for international departure.

Use remaining T-money card balance at airport convenience store. Return card at customer service for 500 KRW deposit refund.

Last-Minute Airport Shopping

Duty-free cosmetics, Korean snacks (honey butter chips, Pepero, dried seaweed), ginseng products. Check weight limits for luggage.
